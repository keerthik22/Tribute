Tribute to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

About Netaji

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha. Known for his daring leadership and revolutionary activities, he played a crucial role in mobilizing the Indian population and advocating for a more aggressive stance against the British. His famous quote "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" continues to inspire generations of Indians.

"One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."

Timeline of Achievements

  • Education

    Subhas Chandra Bose was a brilliant student. He attended the Ravenshaw Collegiate School in Cuttack and later moved to Calcutta (now Kolkata) to study at the Presidency College. He was expelled from the college for his nationalist activities against a British professor but later joined the Scottish Church College and completed his graduation in 1918 with a degree in Philosophy.

  • Civil Services

    In 1919, Subhas Bose went to England to prepare for the Indian Civil Services (ICS) exam. He passed the exam with distinction but resigned from the service in 1921 to join the Indian freedom movement, stating that he could not work under a government that enslaved his country.

  • Entry into Politics

    Joining Indian National Congress (INC): Bose joined the Indian National Congress under the mentorship of Chittaranjan Das, a prominent nationalist leader. He quickly rose through the ranks due to his passion and commitment.

    Mayor of Calcutta: In 1924, he was appointed as the Chief Executive Officer of the Calcutta Municipal Corporation under Chittaranjan Das, who was then the Mayor. This was Bose's first administrative role, where he implemented several reforms.

  • Imprisonment and Exile

    Imprisonment: Due to his involvement in the nationalist movement, Bose was arrested multiple times by the British authorities. His fiery speeches and writings made him a marked man, and he was imprisoned in 1925.

    Exile: After his release in 1927, Bose was elected as the President of the All India Youth Congress and the Secretary of the Bengal State Congress. He actively organized protests and was again arrested and imprisoned. After his release, he was sent to Europe for health reasons and spent the next few years in political exile.

  • Rise in Indian National Congress

    Presidency of INC: Bose returned to India in 1936 and resumed his activities in the Congress. He was elected the President of the Indian National Congress in 1938 and 1939. During his tenure, he advocated for complete and immediate independence from British rule, which was at odds with the Congress's more moderate approach.

  • World War II and the Azad Hind Fauj

    Escape to Germany: During World War II, Bose sought help from foreign powers to gain India's independence. He escaped house arrest in 1941 and traveled to Germany via Afghanistan and the Soviet Union. In Germany, he sought support from the Nazis to fight against the British.

    Formation of Indian Legion: In Germany, he formed the Indian Legion, comprising Indian prisoners of war and expatriates, to fight against the British.

    Move to Southeast Asia: Realizing that Germany was too far from India, Bose moved to Japan in 1943. In Singapore, he took over the leadership of the Indian National Army (INA) or Azad Hind Fauj, which was initially formed by General Mohan Singh with the support of Japan.

  • Death and Legacy

    Death: Subhas Chandra Bose reportedly died on August 18, 1945, in a plane crash in Taiwan while en route to seek further support for India's independence. His death remains controversial, with many conspiracy theories suggesting he survived the crash.

    Legacy: Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose remains one of the most revered freedom fighters in Indian history. His slogan "Jai Hind" is now a salutation in the Indian armed forces and a patriotic cry. His legacy of bravery, sacrifice, and dedication to India's freedom continues to inspire millions.

  • Commemoration and Memorials

    Netaji Bhawan: His ancestral house in Kolkata, known as Netaji Bhawan, is now a museum dedicated to his life and work.

    Statues and Memorials: Numerous statues and memorials across India honor Netaji. His birthday, January 23, is celebrated as Netaji Jayanti or Parakram Divas.

In memory of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, the brave son of India.
